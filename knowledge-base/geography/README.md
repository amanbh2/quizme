# 🌍 Geography Study Notes (BPSC CCE)

Geography questions in BPSC CCE range between **15 to 20 questions**, covering Physical Geography, World/Mapping Geography, and Indian Geography.

---

## 📂 Sub-Subject Structure

1. **`physical-geography/`**
   - *Geomorphology*: Interior of Earth, plate tectonics, earthquakes, volcanoes, rock types, landforms (fluvial, glacial, aeolian, karst).
   - *Climatology*: Atmosphere structure, pressure belts, planetary winds, monsoon mechanism, cyclones (tropical vs. temperate), Koeppen climate classification.
   - *Oceanography*: Ocean relief, salinity, temperature currents (warm vs. cold currents - highly tested), tides.
2. **`world-geography/`**
   - *Mapping*: Major straits (Malacca, Gibraltar, Bab-el-Mandeb, etc.), landlocked countries, major mountain ranges, deserts, lakes, and rivers.
   - *Resources*: Global distribution of minerals (iron ore, copper, petroleum, coal) and major agricultural regions.
3. **`indian-geography/`**
   - *Physiography*: Himalayas, Indo-Gangetic Plains, Peninsular Plateau, Coastal Plains, Islands.
   - *Drainage System*: Himalayan rivers (Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra) vs. Peninsular rivers (Narmada, Tapi, Mahanadi, Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri).
   - *Climate & Vegetation*: Indian monsoon (El Nino/La Nina effects), forest types, soils of India, agriculture (Rabi, Kharif, Zaid crops, Green Revolution).
   - *Resources & Infrastructure*: Mineral belts, major dams, national parks, biosphere reserves, wildlife sanctuaries, transportation (National Highways, ports).

---

## 🎯 BPSC Preparation Strategy

- **Ocean Currents & Straits**: Memorize which ocean currents are warm vs. cold (e.g., Kuroshio, Gulf Stream, Labrador, Humboldt). BPSC often asks to classify them.
- **Mountain Passes**: Keep a map list of Himalayan passes (Sipki La, Nathu La, Bomdila, etc.) and Peninsular passes (Thal Ghat, Bhor Ghat, Pal Ghat).
- **River Tributaries**: Focus on Indus and Ganga tributaries. Confluences of Alaknanda (Panch Prayag) are high yield.
