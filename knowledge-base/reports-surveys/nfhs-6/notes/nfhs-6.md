# 🩺 NFHS-6 — Complete BPSC Study Notes

This note contains fact-checked, high-yield data from the **National Family Health Survey-6 (NFHS-6) 2023-24**, released by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW) on **May 29, 2026**.

---

## 📅 Background & Concepts

*   **Nodal Agency:** International Institute for Population Sciences (IIPS), Mumbai.
*   **Survey Period:** 2023–24.
*   **Release Date:** May 29, 2026.
*   **Scope:** Covers health, family welfare, and nutritional status across all states and Union Territories.
*   **Key Change (Indicator Omission):** Omitted several indicators previously reported in NFHS-5, most notably the **sex ratio at birth** (females per 1000 males born in the last 5 years).

## 🏆 Bihar-Specific NFHS-6 Profile

Bihar's performance in the NFHS-6 represents a crucial transition phase in health and demographics:

### 1. Total Fertility Rate (TFR)
*   **Bihar TFR:** **2.7** (Declined significantly from **3.0** in NFHS-5).
*   **Analysis:** While Bihar's fertility rate is decreasing due to rising female literacy and contraceptive awareness, it is still among the highest in the country and remains above the national average of **2.0**.

### 2. Malnutrition & Child Health
*   **Child Diet Adequacy:** The percentage of children (6–23 months) receiving an adequate diet remains low, reflecting nutrition deficit challenges.
*   **Under-5 Stunting:** Showed a downward trend following national stunting reductions, but remains a focus area.

### 3. Healthcare Infrastructure
*   **Institutional Deliveries:** Sustained upward growth, narrowing the gap with the national average.
*   **Immunization Coverage:** Improved, reflecting strong regional implementation of health programs like Mission Indradhanush.
