# 📈 Bihar Economic Survey 2025-26 — Complete BPSC Study Notes

This note contains fact-checked, high-yield data from the **Bihar Economic Survey 2025-26**, presented in the Bihar Legislative Assembly by the state government in early 2026.

## ⚙️ Sectoral Shares & GSVA (FY 2024-25)

Bihar's Gross State Value Added (GSVA) sectoral shares show a significant rise in the Secondary sector:

*   **Primary Sector (Agriculture & Allied):** **18.3%** (declined from 19.9% in 2023-24).
    *   *Growth driver:* Driven heavily by the crops sub-sector (dominant) and **Fishery** (fastest-growing primary sub-sector at **+9.9%**).
*   **Secondary Sector (Industry):** **26.8%** (a significant increase from 21.1% in 2020-21, reflecting industrial expansion).
    *   *Growth driver:* Dominant contributor is **Construction**, which drives the overall secondary sector real growth of **11.1%** in 2024-25.
*   **Tertiary Sector (Services):** **54.8%** (declined from 58.6% in 2023-24 as industrial output grew).
    *   *Growth driver:* Remains the largest contributor overall.

## 🏛️ Regional Disparities: District rankings (GDDP Per Capita)

BPSC frequently asks about the richest and poorest districts in Bihar based on per capita Gross District Domestic Product (GDDP) at current prices:

### Richest Districts
1.  **Patna:** **₹2,41,220** per capita (exceptionally high due to urban service concentration).
2.  **Begusarai:** **₹1,05,600** per capita (industrial hub/refinery).
3.  **Munger:** **₹93,921** per capita (railway workshop, ITC, manufacturing).

### Poorest Districts
*   **Lowest:** **Sheohar** (lowest per capita GDDP).
*   **Next Poorest:** **Araria** and **Sitamarhi**.
*   *Analysis:* Highlights a stark regional imbalance between the central urban zones and the peripheral northern/eastern districts.

## 🌾 Agriculture, Land & Rural Livelihoods

*   **Crop Intensity:** **152%** (ratio of gross cropped area to net sown area, indicating double-cropping).
*   **Makhana (Fox Nut):** Bihar accounts for **85%** of India's total Makhana production. The Mithila region (Darbhanga, Madhubani, Sitamarhi) is the core hub.
*   **Land Fragmentation (Agriculture Census 2015-16):**
    *   Marginal farmers (holdings < 1 ha) own **57.73%** of the total operational area.
    *   Combined marginal and small (1-2 ha) farmers own **76%** of the total operational area.
*   **Seed Delivery:** Bihar is the first state to introduce **Home Delivery of Seeds** through Bihar Rajya Beej Nigam Ltd. (BRBN).
*   **JEEViKA (Bihar Rural Livelihoods Project):** Launched in **2006** with World Bank support. Operates through women's Self Help Groups (SHGs) to alleviate rural poverty.
*   **VEGFED & Tarkari Sudha:** Bihar State Vegetables and Fruits Development Corporation Ltd. (VEGFED) operates under the **Department of Horticulture**. It runs **Tarkari Sudha** retail outlets using the **Sudha Dairy retail booth model** to sell fresh vegetables directly from farmers.

## ⚡ Infrastructure & Power

*   **Per Capita Energy Consumption:** **374 kWh** (below national average, showing low industrial use).
*   **Power Demand Share:** **Domestic** sector accounts for **47.78%** of demand (the highest share).
*   **Installed Capacity:** **8,339.08 MW** (as of April-September 2025).
    *   *Thermal Power:* Dominates the capacity, contributing **7,743 MW**.
