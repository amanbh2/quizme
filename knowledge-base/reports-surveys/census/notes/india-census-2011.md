# 👥 India Census 2011 — Complete BPSC Study Notes

This note contains fact-checked, high-yield demographic data for the 2011 Census of India, tailored specifically for the BPSC CCE.

---

## 📅 Historical Timeline & Concepts

*   **First Census Attempt (Lord Mayo, 1872):** Non-synchronous and partial census covering major parts of British India.
*   **First Synchronous Census (Lord Ripon, 1881):** First simultaneous, complete all-India census. Conducted decadally ever since.
*   **"Year of the Great Divide" (1921):** The only census in Indian history to record a negative population growth rate (**-0.31%**) due to the 1918 influenza pandemic and widespread famine.
*   **15th Indian Census (2011):** 7th census conducted after Independence (1951 being the first post-independence census).
*   **Census Commissioner:** C. Chandramauli.
*   **Methodology:** India utilizes the **De facto** counting method, which enumerates individuals wherever they are located on census night, rather than where they permanently reside (De jure).
*   **Legal Framework:** Governed by the **Census Act, 1948**, which guarantees that census data is confidential and cannot be used as evidence in a court of law.

## 🏆 State & UT Superlatives

### 1. Population rankings
*   **Most Populous States:**
    1.  Uttar Pradesh (19.98 Crore)
    2.  Maharashtra (11.24 Crore)
    3.  **Bihar** (**10.41 Crore**)
    4.  West Bengal (9.13 Crore)
*   **Least Populous States:**
    1.  Sikkim (6.10 Lakh)
    2.  Mizoram (10.97 Lakh)
    3.  Arunachal Pradesh (13.84 Lakh)
*   **Most Populous UT:** Delhi (1.67 Crore).
*   **Least Populous UT:** Lakshadweep (64,473).

### 2. Population Density
*   **Highest Density States:**
    1.  **Bihar** (**1,106 persons/sq. km**)
    2.  West Bengal (1,028 persons/sq. km)
    3.  Kerala (860 persons/sq. km)
*   **Lowest Density States:**
    1.  Arunachal Pradesh (17 persons/sq. km)
    2.  Mizoram (52 persons/sq. km)
    3.  Sikkim (86 persons/sq. km)
*   **Density UTs:** Highest: Delhi (11,320); Lowest: Andaman & Nicobar (46).

### 3. Sex Ratio
*   **Highest Sex Ratio States:**
    1.  Kerala (**1,084**)
    2.  Tamil Nadu (996)
    3.  Andhra Pradesh (993)
*   **Lowest Sex Ratio States:**
    1.  Haryana (**879**)
    2.  Jammu & Kashmir (889)
    3.  Sikkim (890)
*   **UT Sex Ratio:** Highest: Puducherry (1,037); Lowest: Daman & Diu (618).
*   **Child Sex Ratio (0–6) States:** Highest: Arunachal Pradesh (972); Lowest: Haryana (**834**).

### 4. Literacy Rate
*   **Highest Literacy States:**
    1.  Kerala (**94.00%**)
    2.  Mizoram (91.30%)
    3.  Tripura (87.75%)
*   **Lowest Literacy States:**
    1.  **Bihar** (**61.80%**) — *Lowest in India*
    2.  Arunachal Pradesh (65.38%)
    3.  Rajasthan (66.11%)
*   **UT Literacy:** Highest: Lakshadweep (91.85%); Lowest: Dadra & Nagar Haveli (76.24%).

### 5. Decadal Growth Rate
*   **Highest Growth States:**
    1.  Meghalaya (27.9%)
    2.  Arunachal Pradesh (26.0%)
    3.  **Bihar** (**25.42%**)
*   **Lowest Growth States:**
    1.  Nagaland (**-0.6%**) — *Only state to record a negative growth rate*
    2.  Kerala (4.9%)
    3.  Goa (8.2%)

### 6. Urbanization Level
*   **Most Urbanized States:** Goa (62.2%), Mizoram (52.1%), Tamil Nadu (48.4%).
*   **Least Urbanized States:** Himachal Pradesh (**10.0%**), **Bihar** (**11.3%**), Assam (14.1%).
*   **Most Urbanized UT:** Delhi (97.5%).

## 💼 SC, ST, and Worker Distributions

### 1. Scheduled Castes (SC)
*   **National Percentage:** **16.6%** (20.14 Crore).
*   **Highest Percentage State:** **Punjab** (31.9%).
*   **Largest Absolute SC Population:** Uttar Pradesh (4.13 Crore).

### 2. Scheduled Tribes (ST)
*   **National Percentage:** **8.6%** (10.43 Crore).
*   **Highest Percentage State:** **Mizoram** (94.4%).
*   **Largest Absolute ST Population:** Madhya Pradesh (1.53 Crore).
*   **Note:** Punjab, Haryana, Delhi, Chandigarh, and Puducherry have no notified ST populations.

### 3. Worker Profiles & Reversal
*   **Work Participation Rate:** **39.8%** of total population (Main workers: 29.9%, Marginal: 9.9%).
*   **Key Trend Shift:** Cultivators dropped to **24.6%** of the work force while **Agricultural Labourers rose to 30.0%**, indicating rising rural landlessness across the country.
