# 👥 Bihar Census 2011 — Complete BPSC Study Notes

This note contains fact-checked, high-yield demographic data for the state of Bihar based on the 2011 Census of India.

## 🏆 District-Wise Superlatives & Rankings

### 1. Population & Area
*   **Most Populous Districts:**
    1.  **Patna** (58.38 Lakh) — *Only district with >50 lakh population*
    2.  East Champaran (50.99 Lakh)
    3.  Muzaffarpur (48.01 Lakh)
*   **Least Populous Districts:**
    1.  **Sheikhpura** (6.36 Lakh) — *Least populous district*
    2.  Sheohar (6.56 Lakh)
    3.  Arwal (7.00 Lakh)
*   **Largest District by Area:** **West Champaran** (5,228 sq. km) — *"Champion of Size"*
*   **Smallest District by Area:** **Sheohar** (349 sq. km) — *"Small but Suffocating"*

### 2. Population Density
*   **Highest Density Districts:**
    1.  **Sheohar** (**1,880 persons/sq. km**) — *Smallest area leads to the highest density*
    2.  Patna (1,823 persons/sq. km)
    3.  Darbhanga (1,728 persons/sq. km)
*   **Lowest Density Districts:**
    1.  **Kaimur (Bhabua)** (**488 persons/sq. km**) — *Least densely populated due to hills/forests*
    2.  Jamui (568 persons/sq. km)
    3.  Banki (674 persons/sq. km)

### 3. Decadal Growth Rate (2001–2011)
*   **Highest Growth Districts:**
    1.  **Madhepura** (**31.12%**)
    2.  Kishanganj (30.40%)
    3.  Araria (30.25%)
*   **Lowest Growth Districts:**
    1.  **Gopalganj** (**18.83%**)
    2.  Darbhanga (19.47%)
    3.  Munger (20.21%)

### 4. Sex Ratio (Overall & Child)
*   **Highest Sex Ratio Districts (Overall):**
    1.  **Gopalganj** (**1,021**) — *Only district in Bihar where females outnumber males*
    2.  Siwan (988)
    3.  Saran (954)
*   **Lowest Sex Ratio Districts (Overall):**
    1.  **Munger** (**879**) — *Lowest sex ratio*
    2.  Bhagalpur (880)
    3.  Khagaria (886)
*   **Highest Child Sex Ratio (0–6 years):** **Kishanganj** (**971**)
*   **Lowest Child Sex Ratio (0–6 years):** **Vaishali / Patna** (**904**)

### 5. Literacy Rate (Total & Gender-wise)
*   **Highest Literacy Districts:**
    1.  **Rohtas** (**73.37%**) — *Top in overall, male, and female literacy*
    2.  Patna (70.68%)
    3.  Munger (70.46%)
*   **Lowest Literacy Districts:**
    1.  **Purnia** (**51.08%**) — *Lowest overall literacy in the state*
    2.  Kishanganj (55.46%)
    3.  Katihar (52.24%)
*   **Highest Male Literacy:** **Rohtas** (82.88%).
*   **Lowest Male Literacy:** **Purnia** (59.06%).
*   **Highest Female Literacy:** **Rohtas** (62.97%).
*   **Lowest Female Literacy:** **Saharsa** (**41.68%**).

### 6. Urbanization Level
*   **Highest Urbanized Districts (Percentage):**
    1.  **Patna** (**43.07%**)
    2.  Munger (27.80%)
    3.  Bhagalpur (19.80%)
*   **Lowest Urbanized Districts (Percentage):**
    1.  **Samastipur** (**3.47%**)
    2.  Banka (3.50%)
    3.  Madhubani (3.60%)

## 💼 Scheduled Castes (SC) & Scheduled Tribes (ST) in Bihar

*   **SC Population distribution:**
    *   Total SC population is **15.72%** (1.65 Crore) of the state.
    *   **Highest SC population percentage:** **Gaya** (**30.4%**).
    *   **Lowest SC population percentage:** Kishanganj (6.7%).
*   **ST Population distribution:**
    *   Total ST population is only **1.28%** (13.4 Lakh) of the state.
    *   **Highest ST population percentage:** **West Champaran** (**6.35%**).
    *   **Lowest ST population percentage:** Sheohar (0.01%) / Sheikhpura (0.03%).

## 🗣️ Language & Official Status

*   **Hindi:** The primary official language of Bihar and the most widely spoken.
*   **Maithili:** Second official language of Bihar. It is the only regional language of Bihar **included in the 8th Schedule** of the Constitution (added via the 92nd Amendment in 2003, along with Bodo, Dogri, and Santhali).
*   **Bhojpuri:** Widely spoken in West Bihar (Buxar, Bhojpur, Rohtas, Saran) and has an ongoing demand for inclusion in the 8th Schedule.
*   **Magahi:** Spoken in South Bihar (Gaya, Nalanda, Patna, Nawada).
*   **Angika:** Spoken in Southeast Bihar (Bhagalpur, Banka, Munger).
*   **Bajjika:** Spoken in North-Central Bihar (Muzaffarpur, Vaishali, Sitamarhi).
