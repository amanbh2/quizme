# 👥 Topic 1.2: Census History & Legal Framework — Census Study Notes

This note covers the historical timeline, major milestones, and legal infrastructure governing the decadal Census of India.

---

## 📅 Historical Timeline & Milestones

*   **1872 (First Census Attempt):**
    *   Conducted under **Lord Mayo** (Viceroy).
    *   It was **non-synchronous** (did not occur at the same time across the country) and **partial** (covered only parts of British India).
*   **1881 (First Synchronous Census):**
    *   Conducted under **Lord Ripon** (Viceroy) by Census Commissioner W.W. Plowden.
    *   This was the **first simultaneous, complete all-India census**.
    *   Established the decadal cycle (every 10 years) followed ever since.
*   **1921 ("Year of the Great Divide"):**
    *   The **only** census in Indian history to record a **negative decadal growth rate** of **-0.31%** (a decrease of ~7.7 Lakh people).
    *   **Reason:** The severe 1918 influenza pandemic (Spanish Flu) coupled with widespread famine and cholera/plague outbreaks.
*   **1951 (First Post-Independence Census):**
    *   First census conducted in independent India.

---

## 🚫 State Exclusions

Due to internal security and law-and-order issues, specific states were omitted from certain census rounds and counted subsequently:
1.  **Assam (1981):** Excluded from the 1981 census due to active agitations and unstable conditions.
2.  **Jammu & Kashmir (1991):** Excluded from the 1991 census due to rampant militancy and unrest.

---

## 🏛️ Legal & Administrative Framework

*   **Nodal Authority:**
    *   Conducted by the **Office of the Registrar General and Census Commissioner, India (ORGI)**.
    *   Under the administrative control of the **Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA)**, Government of India.
*   **Governing Law: Census Act, 1948**
    *   Provides the legal basis for conducting the census, appointing enumerators, and defining obligations of citizens to answer questions truthfully.
    *   > [!IMPORTANT]
        > **Confidentiality Clause:** The Act guarantees absolute confidentiality of individual information. Census data **cannot be used as evidence** in a court of law, and is not accessible under the RTI Act or by tax authorities.
