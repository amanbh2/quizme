# 👥 Topic 1.1: Core Concepts & Definitions — Census Study Notes

This note covers the essential definitions and demographic methodologies used in the Census of India, tailored for the BPSC CCE.

---

## 📅 Enumeration Methodologies

*   **De facto Method (Used in India):**
    *   **Definition:** Enumerates individuals wherever they are physically present on the census reference night, regardless of their permanent residence.
    *   **BPSC Context:** Focuses on spatial presence. Leads to transient population capture at their temporary locations.
*   **De jure Method:**
    *   **Definition:** Enumerates individuals at their usual/permanent place of residence, regardless of where they are on the census night.

## 📊 Core Demographic Formulas & Definitions

### 1. Literacy Rate
*   **Definition:** The percentage of the population aged **7 years and above** who can both read and write with understanding in at least one language.
*   **Formula:**
    $$\text{Literacy Rate} = \left( \frac{\text{Number of Literate Persons (Aged 7+)}}{\text{Total Population (Aged 7+)}} \right) \times 100$$
*   > [!NOTE]
    > Prior to the 1991 Census, the literacy rate was calculated for the population aged **5 years and above**.

### 2. Sex Ratio
*   **Definition:** The number of females per 1000 males in the population.
*   **Formula:**
    $$\text{Sex Ratio} = \left( \frac{\text{Number of Females}}{\text{Number of Males}} \right) \times 1000$$

### 3. Child Sex Ratio (0–6 Years)
*   **Definition:** The number of female children per 1000 male children in the age group 0–6 years.
*   **Formula:**
    $$\text{Child Sex Ratio} = \left( \frac{\text{Number of Female Children (0-6)}}{\text{Number of Male Children (0-6)}} \right) \times 1000$$

### 4. Population Density
*   **Definition:** The average number of persons living per unit of area (typically square kilometer).
*   **Formula:**
    $$\text{Population Density} = \frac{\text{Total Population}}{\text{Total Land Area (in sq. km)}}$$

## 💼 Worker Classifications & Categories

The Census divides the population into three major classes based on the duration of work during the reference year:

| Category | Definition / Criteria | Key BPSC Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Main Workers** | Worked for **183 days (6 months) or more** in the year. | Primary economic indicators. |
| **Marginal Workers** | Worked for **less than 183 days (6 months)** in the year. | Measures seasonal/under-employment. |
| **Non-workers** | Did not work at all (students, home-makers, dependents). | Represents dependency ratio. |
