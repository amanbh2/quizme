# 📊 Union Budget 2026-27 — Complete BPSC Study Notes

This note contains fact-checked, high-yield data from the **Union Budget of India 2026-27**, presented in the Lok Sabha by Union Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman on **February 1, 2026**.

---

## 📅 Background & Core Themes

*   **Presented On:** February 1, 2026.
*   **Prepared By:** Ministry of Finance, Government of India.
*   **Vision/Theme:** **"Viksit Bharat"** (Developed India), focusing on three primary *kartavyas* (duties):
    1.  **Accelerating Economic Growth:** Enhancing productivity, competitiveness, and building economic resilience.
    2.  **Fulfilling Aspirations:** Building human capacity and strengthening partnerships in prosperity.
    3.  **Inclusive Development:** Ensuring access to resources and opportunities across all regions and sectors.

## 💵 Taxation Proposals & Changes

### 1. Direct Income Tax
*   **Tax Slabs:** There are no changes to the income tax slabs for the assessment year 2026-27.
*   **Full Tax Rebate:** A full income tax rebate is highlighted for individuals earning up to **₹12.75 Lakh** annually under the New Tax Regime.

### 2. Remittance & Travel TCS Reforms
*   **Overseas Tour Packages:** Tax Collected at Source (TCS) rate reduced to **2%** (down from previous slabs of 5% and 20%).
*   **LRS Educational & Medical Remittances:** TCS rate reduced to **2%** (down from 5% for remittances above ₹7 lakh).

### 3. Financial Markets & Corporate Tax
*   **Securities Transaction Tax (STT):** STT rate on futures and options/equity trading increased from 0.1% to **0.15%**.
*   **Share Buybacks:** Now taxed as capital gains in the hands of recipients, with additional taxes targeting promoters to prevent tax avoidance.
*   **Tax Holidays Extended:** Extended for specific sectors including IFSC units and foreign companies providing global cloud services.
