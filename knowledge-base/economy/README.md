# 📈 Indian Economy & Planning Study Notes (BPSC CCE)

Economy accounts for **10 to 15 questions** in BPSC CCE. The focus is heavily split between core conceptual questions (inflation, monetary policy) and factual data (Union Budget, Economic Survey, Schemes).

---

## 📂 Sub-Subject Structure

1. **`core-concepts/`**
   - *National Income*: GDP, GNP, NDP, NNP, real vs. nominal GDP.
   - *Inflation*: CPI, WPI, effects of inflation on creditors/debtors, measures to control inflation.
   - *Monetary & Fiscal Policy*: RBI's quantitative tools (Repo, Reverse Repo, CRR, SLR, Bank Rate) vs. qualitative tools; government revenue, expenditure, and deficits (fiscal, primary, revenue).
   - *Financial Markets*: Banking structure, NBFCs, NPAs, insolvency (IBC), capital/money market basics (SEBI).
2. **`planning-schemes/`**
   - *Economic Planning*: History of planning, Five Year Plans (targets, achievements, models like Harrod-Domar, Mahalanobis), transition to NITI Aayog (structure, indices).
   - *Welfare Schemes*: Central government schemes (social security, health, employment like MGNREGA, PM-KISAN).
3. **`budget-survey/`**
   - *Union Budget*: Current year revenue receipts, capital expenditure, fiscal deficit target, new policy announcements, tax slabs.
   - *Economic Survey*: Key sector performance growth rates (agriculture, industry, services), trade balance, major challenges.

---

## 🎯 BPSC Preparation Strategy

- **Five Year Plans**: BPSC regularly asks about the main themes, slogans, and models of specific Five Year Plans (especially the 1st, 2nd, 5th, 11th, and 12th plans).
- **RBI Monetary Policy Tools**: Make sure you understand how raising or lowering CRR/SLR/Repo affects liquidity, inflation, and rupee value.
- **Budget Figures**: Note down the exact fiscal deficit target (as a percentage of GDP) and sector allocations for the current financial year.
